The return of the garage queer-punk trio, which was of limited sensation in its time, formed at Woodstock in 2017 and disbanded in 2022 immediately after the release of the third long play due to the inability to support their existence with music (people really, unfortunately, would rather spend 5 bucks on coffee than on an album on bandcamp). But it turns out that Jason Momoa (you read that correctly) is a big fan of the band. The actor helped get the group signed to Epitaph and actually sponsored the recording of their fourth album. 

Still the same very talented original mixture of everything we love - there’s a little bit of Albini, and RATM, and Iggy, and the Beastie Boys. This time there was more nu metal, which only benefited the album.
