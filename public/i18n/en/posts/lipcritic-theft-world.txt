Favorites with a second long play. The group is moving further away from the sound of my favorite release of theirs - Kill Lip Critic, increasing the bias towards noise electronics, but the signature twitchiness is still there. The theme of the release is interesting - it is based on the real story of an attempt by a fan of the band (who was looking for secret signs in the work of a fairly niche group) to steal the identity of the vocalist. 

The editors of the world's best music critical media outlet, Put Your Guns Down, Scum must warn that when you try to steal our identity, you become scum.
